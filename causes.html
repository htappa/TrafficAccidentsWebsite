Accident Causes

Traffic accidents can occur for a wide variety of reasons, usually associated with a particular action taken by the driver. The bar chart below illustrates accident causes by showing the total number of accidents that occured per driver action. There are two additional layers that can be applied to this chart via the drop-down menu: Road Conditions and Light Conditions.

The Road Conditions show the state of the roads at the time of the accident, whether they were dry, wet, muddy, etc. The Light Conditions show the lighting levels at the time of the accident, indicating whether the accident occured in the daylight, at dawn/dusk, or in dark conditions with street lights (lighted) or without street lights (unlighted).


Below is a Sankey diagram, which is a type of flow diagram where the width of the lines is proportional to flow quantity. In this case, the diagram shows the main outcomes of the accidents (Traffic Accident Offense) in relation to the the causes (Driver Action) which led to it, with the size of the lines emphasizing the primary contributors to the accident.
